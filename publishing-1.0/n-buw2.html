Tagging Ordinal Numbers

Volume and edition numbers are frequently given as ordinal numbers (first, second, third) and abbreviated with a “th” or “rd” either directly following the number or in a superscript (<sup>) following the number. Each publisher or archive will need to decide whether or not these suffixes are part of the element’s content.

  • Archives and publishers who wish to regularize content for searching can eliminate these extra characters and tag the edition as <edition>4<edition>.
  • Archives who wish record each detail of the printed volume exactly can retain these extra characters: <edition>4<edition><sup>th</sup>.
